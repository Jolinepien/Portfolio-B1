Expertise Areas

 

In this section I reflect on my development in the expertise areas. You can read this section in order, or use the above menu to scroll down directly to the expertise area you want to read.

 

The images doubles as progress bar for the development the expertise area compared to the other areas. Hover above or click on the images to what they are.

 

Creativity & Aesthetics

At the beginning of the year I had basic knowledge about creativity and aesthetics, but I was not good in things like brainstorming. This has changed during this year.

 

In the last quartile I had to come up with a product within a companyâ€™s profile. During this brainstorm I noticed I was a lot more confident and the ideas I had where more innovative than at the beginning of the year. I have also made a lot of digital sketches. This way I would get a intuition for what looks good.

 

It is important for me as a designer to have developed this competency, since it helps to come up with unique designs and to develop them in such a way that it can be sold.

 

User & Society

Due to my interest in social justice I have already been active with understanding society. During this year I have learnt more.

 

In the course Intercultural Design I have learnt to analyse cultures. This helps to understand the wishes of society. It helps me as a designer, since helps me to base my choices on the characteristics of the culture I am designing for.

 

I have also learnt have to do proper user tests. This is very important for me as designer, since I  will not be designing for me, but for people with other experiences. User tests help to understand the desires of the user. During project 1, I have done 3 user tests in three different phases to see whether the product suits the market.

 

Technology & Realization

During the last year I have learnt quite a lot in technology and realization. This is partly due to the fact that I did not now a lot about this expertise area to begin with.

 

I have learnt how to wire technology with and without code. This is useful for my later career as a designer, because I need to know how to make my products work for a prototype, before I can start manufacturing. It is also useful, since I am now familiar with jargon so I can talk to electrical engineers.

 

Another thing I have done a lot this year is making different kinds of prototypes. This helps me to understand when it is useful to show what with my prototype.

 

Math, Data & Computing

In the first bachelor year there are a lot of obligated courses which contribute to math, data and computing. Since I learn this rather fast, this expertise area developed a lot during this year.

 

The most important thing I have learnt is a more abstract way of thinking. With this way of thinking it is easier to make more 'objective' decisions. This is important as a designer, since it helps making design decisions, with less bias for what I prefer myself.

 

During the last two semesters I have learnt four (three and a half) programming languages: Arduino, Processing, MATLAB and the basics of Python. These languages will help me to develop as a designer, since I can now make better decisions what to use to get the best result.

 

Business & Entrepreneurship

This year, I have learnt a little in business and entrepreneurship. I have followed one elective in this area: Designing with and for multiple stakeholders.

 

I have learnt how to evaluate the values from a company and what values a product offers to the stakeholders. Furthermore, I have learnt how to use this information to make a design decision. Lastly, I have learnt to analyse the place of the companies in an ecosystem and who are relevant stakeholders.

 

During project 1 I have checked the business plan after my group mates made it. This helped me to learn what makes a good business plan, but you also notice what should be different if something is wrong.

 

Business and Entrepreneurship is important for me as designer, since it helps to understand design opportunities. Moreover, it will help to bring a product to the market and make an impact on society.
